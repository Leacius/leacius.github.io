MISTT: Automated Broadcast Video Analysis System for Table Tennis

Tang-Chen Chang

Duen-Chian Jheng

Hsuan-Ya Liang

Bill Louis Harchan

Te-Cheng Wu

Hung-Kuo Chu

Min-Chun Hu

*National Tsing Hua University*

Abstract

To efficiently analyze table tennis players' tactics in international competitions, this study presents a framework that automatically recognizes fine-grained stroke actions from broadcast videos. Our framework starts with an action segmentation module to extract stroke clips based on ball placements. The player regions along each stroke clip are cropped for the following action recognition module. Broadcast-view table tennis stroke action recognition is challenging due to small inter-class spatio-temporal differences and limited available data. To address these issues, we introduce the adaptive temporal different model with a Transformer encoder (ATDT) to capture the nuanced motion and key spatio-temporal information across stroke actions in various lengths. To further separate the latent features of nuanced stroke actions and alleviate the overfitting problem, we employ contrastive loss with class-based positives and negatives. Additionally, we collect the MISTT dataset, comprising 18 international table tennis competition videos with annotations in 28 fine-grained categories defined by professional coaches, to evaluate the performance of our framework in analyzing table tennis competitions. Experimental results demonstrate that the proposed method outperforms the baseline model by 33.22%.
